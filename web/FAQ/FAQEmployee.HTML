  • Student Reaction Survey(SRS)

From the past several years we have seen a striking increase in attempts to evaluate collegeteaching. SRS (Student Reaction Survey) is one of the widely used tools to evaluate college teaching. Doing good evaluation is like doing good research. In both cases, you are trying to answer some important questions about an important topic. The key to doing both activities well is

  • Identifying the right questions to ask.
  • Figuring out how to answer them.

What are the key questions in the evaluation of teaching?

Basically they are:

  • "How well am I teaching?"
  • "Which aspects of my teaching are good and which are need to be improved?"

The first question attempts to provide a global assessment, while the second is analytical and diagnostic in character.

A second reason to evaluate is to document the quality of one's works for others. For every professional there are others who need to know the quality of his work and the level of expertise he has in the field. This can be achieved by a continuous evaluation of his work.

As the intended beneficiaries of all teaching, students are in a unique position to help their teachers in the evaluation process.

Features of SRS:

  • Feedback in teaching-learning process in class committee.
  • Academic issues brought out by student secretaries to board of academic course and research.
  • Informal and Unstructured feedback in classroom.
  • Teacher's course evaluation by student.

Processes of SRS:

  • At the close of semester, students fill-up On-line Teacher Course Feedback form (for every course with any number of student).
  • Evaluation week is declared in the Academic Calendar.
  • Student give response by way of their comments.
  • Teachers are not involved at any point in the process.
